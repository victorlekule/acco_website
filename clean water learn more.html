Health & Environmental Project

Clean Water Access Project

Drilling new boreholes and installing robust filtration systems to provide safe, clean drinking water to remote villages.

The Crisis of Contaminated Water

Access to clean water is the single most critical factor in preventing disease and promoting health. Many remote communities in Mtwara still rely on unsafe surface water sources, leading to high rates of cholera, typhoid, and other waterborne illnesses, especially among young children. This project directly addresses the root cause of these health crises.

Our Solution: Boreholes and Filtration

  • **Deep Borehole Drilling:** Accessing sustainable, underground aquifers for reliable water supply.
  • **Integrated Filtration Systems:** Installing simple, durable technology to ensure the water meets safety standards.
  • **Community Ownership:** Training local teams on maintenance and repairs to ensure long-term sustainability of the system.

Expected Impact

4 Villages

Receiving permanent, clean water access.

75% Reduction

Expected in waterborne disease cases.
